Picture pattern▸

Make a pattern from a picture instead of the motif — three house pictures are always here to play with. Drag the three dots in Fine-tune to choose what repeats; the picked area is pulled into the tiling shape. Hexagon is the honeycomb kaleidoscope (mirrored or spun slices); Pentagon weaves the Cairo pentagonal tiling; Misc holds the quieter styles — triangle, mirror, kaleidoscope, pinwheel. Uploads stay saved here — remove one with its ×.

Outline▸

Draws a line on the motif (turn it on with the switch in this section’s header). Around the shape traces one clean line hugging the outside of the whole motif — it doesn’t cut into the fill or show the seams between pieces. Along each edge instead strokes every edge of every piece, right on the outline you drew with the fine-tune points.

Logo▸

Places the Lavoët bellboy logo in the centre of a swatch — on the motif pattern it replaces one motif or a block of four; on a picture pattern it sits on top, with a blurred patch or a solid colour behind it for legibility. It shows on swatches only — the downloadable tile stays a clean repeat. “Upload emblem…” brings in your own figure, “Edit emblem…” opens the editor on the current one (your upload, or the house bellboy itself), and “Create wordmark…” lets you type your own text in place of LAVOËT 1869 — once you have your own, cards appear here to switch between yours and the house versions.

Saved designs▸

Your work is kept in this browser automatically — close the tab and reopen to pick up exactly where you left off (only Reset goes back to the original trunk motif). “Save current design” pins a copy, named with the moment (click the ✎ to rename). Hover a thumbnail to see that design enlarged. The ten most recent are listed here — “Browse all” opens the rest. Autosaves are the dashed ones, a safety net keeping your last twenty versions; the three newest are listed and “Earlier autosaves” opens the rest. They’re taken once your edits settle, at most every 30 seconds, and only when the design differs from one already kept — so they mark real stages of the work rather than every tweak.

Saved locally in this browser — nothing leaves your device.
